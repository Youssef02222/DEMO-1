Quality Management

What is Quality Management

QUALITY PLANNING

The process of identifying the quality standards relevant to the project and deciding how to meet them.

QUALITY IMPROVEMENT

The purposeful change of a process to improve the confidence or reliability of the outcome.

QUALITY CONTROL

The continuing effort to uphold a process’s integrity and reliability in achieving an outcome.

QUALITY ASSURANCE

The systematic or planned actions necessary to offer sufficient reliability so that a particular service or product will meet the specified requirements.

Mission & Vision

The mission of MQS at medical laboratories is to ensure that laboratory results are accurate, reliable, and meet the required quality standards and guidelines. This includes implementing and maintaining a quality management system, implementing quality control measures, maintaining accurate records, regularly evaluating laboratory performance, providing ongoing training and education to laboratory personnel, and ensuring excellent customer service.


The vision of MQS in medical laboratories is to achieve a culture of continuous improvement and excellence in laboratory operations, ultimately leading to the highest quality of patient care. Our company program aims to establish and maintain a system of procedures, processes, and activities that promote a culture of excellence, innovation, continuous learning and Consistent adherence to regulatory requirements.

Our Approach

01   MQS approach at medical laboratories is a systematic and proactive process that ensures laboratory results are accurate, reliable, and consistent with the highest quality standards.

02   An essential component of the MQS approach is Quality control measures including calibration and verification of equipment, validation and monitoring of test methods, and monitoring of analytical results.

03   Another important aspect is comprehensive quality assurance program that includes monitoring, evaluating, and reporting of laboratory performance, identifying and resolving problems, and maintaining accurate records.

04   Moreover, Continuous Improvement is essential for maintaining the highest levels of quality including ongoing training and education for laboratory personnel, regular evaluation of laboratory performance, and the use of advanced technology and best practices to achieve continuous improvement

05   In addition, Customer Focus approach is crucial for maintaining high levels of customer satisfaction including effective communication with healthcare providers and patients, timely and accurate reporting of laboratory results, and responding quickly to inquiries and concerns.

06   In conclusion Regulatory Compliance is a critical aspect of the MQS approach to ensures that laboratory operations meet or exceed regulatory requirements and maintain accreditation
